New Home Construction Requirements

September 2024 Update

Works in conjunction with Blue Mountain Declaration of Protective Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions and BMPOA Bylaws (available on BMPOA.org under "Homeowners").

All submissions/questions go to ARC Chair via "Contact Us" on BMPOA "Homeowners" page. Include Name, Email, Property Address. Contractors must include sponsoring owner.

Pre-Construction Requirements

MANDATORY: Before applying for any Warren County building permit or zoning variance, owner must notify ARC Chair. Submit in writing via return-receipt mail.

ARC Response Timeline:

ARC Chair (or designee) will respond in writing within 30 days with:

  1. Approval
  2. Disapproval (with reasons)
  3. Request for additional information (owner then has 30 days to supply; ARC has 30 days to respond)

Note: If no response in 30 days, deemed approved.

Required Documentation:

  • Building site plan with detailed plans
  • Materials list
  • Tree removal plan

Construction may not begin until all plans are approved in writing by ARC. Any deviation from approved plans risks legal action.

Construction Siting

  • Construction must be sited unobtrusively—use natural flora to screen from access roads
  • Only remove trees needed for foundation, septic field, and driveway unless extra clearing is approved
  • All exterior work (painting, siding) must be completed within one year of start

Building Requirements

Permitted Structures:

  • One detached single-family dwelling per lot
  • One private garage per lot
  • One accessory building per lot

Exterior Materials:

  • Approved: Wood or wood-look siding (cedar lap, HardiPlank, solid-surface synthetics)
  • Approved: Solid log homes
  • Prohibited: Vinyl siding
  • Note: ARC may grant exceptions case-by-case

Colors:

  • Required: Muted, nature-reflective (greens, browns, tans)
  • Prohibited: Bright/vibrant colors

Fire Safety:

Fire-resistant materials strongly advised for exterior (roof, siding, decking).

Building Setbacks

  • Route 638: ≥ 75 ft from centerline
  • Internal BMPOA roads: ≥ 70 ft from centerline of any internal road/right-of-way
  • Side lines: ≥ 20 ft from each side line
  • Rear line: ≥ 25 ft from rear line of any lot

Note: ARC/Board may grant exceptions to side/rear setbacks.

Dwelling Requirements

  • Minimum size: 1,000 sq ft living space
  • Manufactured homes: Must meet Class A design criteria
  • Garage/accessory building: Must match primary structure's design and materials
  • Tree disposal: Trees felled during construction must be staged unobtrusively or removed
  • Scenic clearing: ARC may allow limited scenic view clearing but not extensive clearing
  • Outhouses: No permanent outside outhouses allowed

Building on an Unpaved Road

Lot developer must construct roadway within platted easement to BMPOA specifications:

  • 3″ compacted #3 gravel base + 2″ crusher run surface
  • 10 ft width between ditches
  • Proper grading/crowning
  • Culverts at low points

Once inspected/accepted by BMPOA Road Committee, maintenance assumed by BMPOA.

Accessory Building Requirements & Restrictions

One accessory building per 1-acre lot (other than a garage) if:

  • Complies with Warren County setback/size/height ordinances
  • Matches primary structure in style/materials
  • Pre-approved in writing by ARC

Warren County Definitions:

  • Building: Any roofed structure supported by walls/columns
  • Permit Exception: Accessory structures < 200 sq ft in side/rear yard do not require County permit but still need ARC approval
  • Height Limit: 12 ft average height (average of eaves to ridge and wall height)

Setback Requirements:

  • Cannot be placed forward of primary residence's front plane
  • ≥ 50 ft from front property line or any public/private road
  • ≥ 10 ft from side lot lines

Special Considerations:

  • Nonconforming Structures: Pre-1992 accessory buildings are grandfathered by County but require ARC pre-approval to avoid covenant violations
  • Foundations & Additions: Accessory buildings (on pilings, skids, slabs) must be in side/rear yard
  • Within 4 ft of primary structure: Considered an addition: requires County permit and must meet primary structure setbacks/height

Important: ARC approval required even after County approval; violations subject to legal action.

Fence Requirements

  • Only split-rail fences allowed
  • Submit design/materials plan to ARC for written approval
  • Exceptions case-by-case based on compatibility

Failure to Comply

  1. BMPOA/ARC notifies owner in writing of violation and required corrective action
  2. Owner has 60 days to correct or 20 days to submit alternative plan/timetable
  3. ARC has 30 days to approve/disapprove alternative plan; no response = deemed approved
  4. If alternative denied, owner has 60 days to comply
  5. Appeals to Arbitration Committee within 30 days; if denied, owner must comply within 30 days or Board may pursue legal action
