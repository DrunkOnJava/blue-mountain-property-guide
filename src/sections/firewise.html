FireWise Safety Pamphlet

Virginia FireWise Communities Program

BMPOA partners with the Virginia Department of Forestry on fire mitigation and evacuation planning. This guide provides detailed recommendations for creating defensible space around structures and reducing wildfire risk.

Defensible Space Zones

Zone 1 (0–5 ft from structure)

  • Hardscape only: Gravel, stone, concrete
  • No combustible materials of any kind
  • Keep area clean and well-maintained

Zone 2 (5–30 ft from structure)

  • Prune tree canopies to maintain 10 ft vertical clearance
  • Remove dead/downed vegetation regularly
  • Plant low-flammability, well-watered vegetation
  • Space plants to prevent fire from spreading
  • Use native, drought-resistant species when possible

Zone 3 (30–100 ft from structure)

  • Thin forested areas; space trees at least twice their mature height apart
  • Remove ladder fuels (brush, low branches)
  • Clear leaf litter frequently
  • Maintain a 30 ft ember-resistant zone beyond structure
  • Create fuel breaks along slopes

Home Hardening

Roof & Attic

  • Roofing: Class A fire-resistant roofing (metal, asphalt shingles)
  • Vents: Screen vents with 1/16″ metal mesh
  • Gutters: Install gutter guards and clean regularly
  • Chimneys: Install spark arrestors

Exterior Walls

  • Siding: Non-combustible siding (fiber cement, brick, stone)
  • Sealing: Seal gaps around eaves, windows, doors
  • Exterior trim: Use fire-resistant materials

Windows & Doors

  • Glass: Double-pane tempered glass
  • Shutters: Fire-resistant shutters where appropriate
  • Weather-stripping: Seal gaps to block embers
  • Screen doors: Use fine mesh screens

Vegetation Management

Plant Selection

  • Replace high-flammability shrubs (juniper, arborvitae)
  • Use native, low-flammability alternatives (salvia, bee balm)
  • Choose plants with high moisture content
  • Avoid plants with volatile oils

Maintenance Practices

  • Keep woodpiles ≥ 30 ft from structures, elevated on metal racks
  • Remove dead branches within 6 ft of stovepipe outlets and chimneys
  • Mow grass regularly during dry seasons
  • Prune tree branches to 10 ft above ground
  • Remove dead vegetation promptly

Water Features

  • Install irrigation systems for landscaping
  • Maintain swimming pools or water features as fire suppression resources
  • Ensure water access for fire departments

Evacuation Planning

Develop a Written Plan

  • Designate meeting points outside the fire zone
  • Map multiple escape routes
  • Assign family roles and responsibilities
  • Include provisions for pets and livestock

Prepare "Go Bags"

  • Pet carriers readily accessible
  • N95 masks for smoke protection
  • Essential documents (ID, insurance, medical records)
  • Medications and first aid supplies
  • Emergency food and water (3-day supply)
  • Battery-powered radio and flashlights

Stay Informed

  • Sign up for Warren County reverse-9-1-1 alerts at warrencountyva.gov
  • Monitor local emergency broadcasts
  • Follow BMPOA Facebook groups for community updates

Community Support

  • Maintain a volunteer list to assist neighbors with limited mobility
  • Share evacuation plans with neighbors
  • Coordinate with local emergency services
