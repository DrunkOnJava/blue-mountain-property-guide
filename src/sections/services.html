V. COMMUNITY SERVICES & AMENITIES

A. ROADS & WINTER WEATHER GUIDELINES

Blue Mountain's mountain road system requires special maintenance and seasonal preparation. Our community roads are maintained through the Sanitary District with careful attention to mountain conditions and weather challenges.

Warren County waste management convenience sites serving Blue Mountain residents

Road Maintenance

  • Regular Maintenance: Grading, pothole repair, and drainage management
  • Seasonal Preparation: Pre-winter inspection and preparation
  • Emergency Repairs: Storm damage and washout repairs
  • Long-term Planning: Road surface improvements and upgrades
  • Traffic Control: Speed limit enforcement and signage

Winter Weather Management

  • Snow Plowing: Primary roads cleared first, secondary roads as conditions permit
  • Salt/Sand Application: Applied to steep grades and problem areas
  • Priority System: Emergency access roads maintained first
  • Resident Responsibility: Individual driveways not maintained by community
  • Equipment Storage: Snow removal equipment stored at community facility

Winter Driving Guidelines

Vehicle Preparation

  • Snow tires or all-season tires with good tread
  • Tire chains available for extreme conditions
  • Emergency kit including blankets, food, and water
  • Full fuel tank and windshield washer fluid
  • Ice scraper and snow brush

Mountain Driving Safety

  • Reduce speed on steep or winding sections
  • Increase following distance in winter conditions
  • Use lower gears on steep descents
  • Avoid sudden braking or acceleration
  • Turn on headlights during snow or fog

Winter Emergency Procedures

If you become stranded on community roads during winter weather:

  1. Stay with your vehicle unless walking to safety is clearly safer
  2. Call 911 if anyone needs medical attention
  3. Contact BMPOA emergency line for road conditions
  4. Use cell phone sparingly to preserve battery
  5. Stay warm but ensure adequate ventilation

Warren County waste collection routes and disposal facility locations for Blue Mountain residents

Blue Mountain residents are served by Warren County's waste management system, which provides regular collection service and access to county disposal facilities.

Regular Collection Service

  • Collection Day: Weekly pickup on designated collection day
  • Collection Time: Place containers at road by 7:00 AM
  • Container Requirements: County-approved containers only
  • Weight Limits: Maximum 50 pounds per container
  • Hazardous Materials: Not accepted in regular collection
  • Holiday Schedule: Modified schedule during major holidays

Recycling Program

  • Materials Accepted: Paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, metal cans
  • Collection Schedule: Bi-weekly recycling pickup
  • Preparation: Clean containers, remove caps and lids
  • Container Type: County-provided recycling bins
  • Location: Place at roadside with regular trash

County Disposal Facilities

Warren County Landfill

  • Location: 1400 Ben Salem Road, Front Royal, VA
  • Hours: Monday-Saturday 7:00 AM - 4:00 PM
  • Services: Household trash, yard waste, construction debris
  • Fees: Tipping fees apply for large loads
  • Requirements: Photo ID and proof of Warren County residency

Recycling Center

  • Location: Multiple drop-off locations throughout county
  • Materials: Extended recycling beyond curbside pickup
  • Electronics: Computer and electronic equipment recycling
  • Hazardous Waste: Special collection events throughout year

Special Disposal Items

Yard Waste

  • Seasonal collection with regular trash service
  • Must be in biodegradable bags or bundled
  • County landfill accepts unlimited yard waste
  • Community composting area available

Large Items

  • Furniture and appliances require special pickup
  • Schedule through Warren County waste management
  • Additional fees may apply
  • County landfill accepts with tipping fees

Hazardous Materials

  • Paint, chemicals, automotive fluids
  • Special collection events held quarterly
  • Some materials accepted at county facility
  • Never place in regular trash collection

C. INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDERS

Blue Mountain's mountain location presents unique challenges for internet and communication services. Several providers offer service to the area with varying levels of coverage and reliability.

Cable/DSL Providers

Shenandoah Telecommunications (Shentel)

  • Service Type: Cable internet and phone
  • Coverage: Most of Blue Mountain subdivision
  • Speeds: Up to 100 Mbps download speeds available
  • Reliability: Generally reliable with occasional weather-related outages
  • Contact: 1-800-743-6835

CenturyLink (Lumen)

  • Service Type: DSL and fiber internet
  • Coverage: Limited availability in subdivision
  • Speeds: Variable based on distance from equipment
  • Reliability: Depends on copper line condition
  • Contact: 1-800-244-1111

Satellite Internet

Starlink (SpaceX)

  • Service Type: Low Earth orbit satellite internet
  • Coverage: Available throughout Blue Mountain
  • Speeds: 50-250 Mbps typical speeds
  • Equipment: Requires dish installation and clear sky view
  • Weather: Minimal weather interference

HughesNet

  • Service Type: Geostationary satellite internet
  • Coverage: Available throughout Blue Mountain
  • Speeds: Up to 25 Mbps download speeds
  • Data Limits: Monthly data allowances apply
  • Weather: Can be affected by severe weather

Cellular/Mobile Hotspot

Verizon Wireless

  • Coverage: Good coverage throughout most of Blue Mountain
  • Service: 4G LTE and 5G where available
  • Data Plans: Unlimited and metered options
  • Equipment: Mobile hotspot devices available

AT&T Wireless

  • Coverage: Variable coverage, some dead zones
  • Service: 4G LTE service primary
  • Fixed Wireless: Home internet via cellular available
  • Reliability: May require signal boosters in some areas

Service Selection Recommendations

Factors to Consider

  • Property Location: Distance from equipment affects DSL speeds
  • Tree Coverage: Dense forest may affect satellite service
  • Usage Needs: Streaming, work-from-home requirements
  • Backup Service: Consider multiple providers for reliability
  • Installation: Some services require professional installation

Best Practices

  • Test service reliability before canceling existing service
  • Consider seasonal variations in performance
  • Ask neighbors about their service experiences
  • Understand data limits and overage charges
  • Plan for power outages affecting equipment

D. ROAD & WINTER WEATHER GUIDELINES

Winter Road Maintenance

  • Salt Prohibition: Salt use prohibited on BMPOA gravel roads (damages roadbed)
  • Traction Aid: Blue barrels of gravel chips are placed on steep hills for traction
  • Tire Chains: Recommended—practice installation before winter
  • Snow Plowing: Begins when 4″ of snow accumulates at a designated mountain location

Emergency Road Situations

Downed Trees

Residents may clear safely or report via BMPOA.org "Contact Us"

Downed Power Lines

Critical Safety: If a line falls on your vehicle, stay inside; if exiting, jump clear and move ≥ 50 ft away

Contact: Rappahannock Electric Cooperative (REC) at (800) 552-3904 or report at myrec.coop/outagecenter

Road Condition Issues

For potholes, washouts, signage issues: email bmpoaroads@gmail.com with name, email, location, photos

Fire Trail Road

Not owned/maintained by BMPOA—contact VDGIF Region 4 for issues

E. SANITARY DISTRICT SERVICES

BMPOA Sanitary District Responsibilities

As a Sanitary District, BMPOA oversees:

  • Water supply & sewerage
  • Garbage disposal
  • Maintenance of certain roads (gravel roads; culverts; high-water crossings)
  • Community building (Lodge) upkeep

Funding Structure

Collection: Taxes collected by Warren County (5% collection fee + $350 annual)

No Liens: No liens on individual properties

Benefits of Sanitary District Status

  • Access to state/federal disaster relief
  • Uniform assessment structure
  • Improved credit status
