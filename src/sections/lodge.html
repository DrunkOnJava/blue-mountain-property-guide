VII. THE LODGE

A. LODGE LOCATION & FEATURES

The Blue Mountain Lodge - a beautiful mountain facility available for community events and private rentals

The Blue Mountain Lodge serves as the community's central gathering place and event venue. Located at 540 Cliff Rd, Linden, VA 22642 in the heart of the subdivision, the Lodge provides a beautiful mountain setting for both community functions and private celebrations.

Facility Features

  • Spacious main hall with mountain views
  • Full kitchen facilities for catering
  • Stone fireplace for year-round ambiance
  • Outdoor deck overlooking the valley
  • Parking area for multiple vehicles
  • Tables and chairs for various event sizes

Typical Uses

  • Community meetings and board meetings
  • Wedding receptions and anniversaries
  • Birthday parties and family reunions
  • Holiday celebrations and potluck dinners
  • Memorial services and life celebrations
  • Seasonal community gatherings

The scenic overlook view from the Lodge area, showcasing the spectacular mountain vistas available to event guests

B. RENTAL INFORMATION & PRICING

The Lodge is available for rental by Blue Mountain property owners and their guests. Rental fees help maintain the facility and support community operations.

Rental Rates

Event Type Property Owner Rate Guest Rate Deposit

What's Included

  • Use of main hall and kitchen facilities
  • Tables and chairs (seating for up to 60)
  • Basic kitchen equipment and utensils
  • Parking area access
  • Outdoor deck usage

Renter Provides

  • All food, beverages, and catering
  • Linens, decorations, and special equipment
  • Setup and cleanup of event areas
  • Trash removal and facility cleaning
  • Any additional chairs beyond 60-person capacity

Booking Process

  1. Check Availability: Contact the Lodge Committee to verify date availability
  2. Submit Application: Complete the Lodge rental application form
  3. Pay Deposit: Submit required deposit with signed agreement
  4. Pre-Event Meeting: Meet with Lodge Committee for facility walkthrough
  5. Final Payment: Pay remaining balance before event date
  6. Post-Event Inspection: Complete cleanup and facility inspection

C. LODGE USAGE GUIDELINES

To ensure the Lodge remains a beautiful and functional community asset, all renters must follow established usage guidelines and procedures.

Facility Care

  • Cleaning: Leave facility in same condition as found
  • Damage: Report any damage immediately to Lodge Committee
  • Furniture: Do not move heavy furniture without permission
  • Decorations: No nails, staples, or permanent attachments
  • Kitchen: Clean all used appliances and utensils
  • Trash: All garbage must be removed by renter

Event Guidelines

  • Hours: Events must end by 11:00 PM
  • Noise: Respect neighboring properties
  • Parking: Use designated parking areas only
  • Capacity: Maximum 60 people for seated events
  • Alcohol: Permitted with responsible service
  • Smoking: No smoking inside facility

Special Considerations

Weather Events

The Lodge is available year-round, but winter access may be affected by snow conditions. Renters should have alternative plans for severe weather events and may need to assist with snow removal for access.

Emergency Procedures

Emergency contact information is posted in the Lodge kitchen. In case of emergency, contact 911 first, then notify the Lodge Committee chair. A first aid kit is located in the kitchen area.

Key Access

Lodge keys are provided before your event and must be returned within 24 hours. Lost keys will result in lock replacement charges. Unauthorized key duplication is prohibited.

Lodge Committee Contact

For reservations, questions, or to report facility issues, contact the Lodge Committee through the main BMPOA contact information listed in Section IX of this guide.
