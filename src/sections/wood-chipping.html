III. WOOD-CHIPPING PROGRAM

A. ANNUAL CHIPPING & FIRE MITIGATION

All property owners within BMPOA Sanitary District are eligible for free wood chipping annually to reduce fire risk. This community-wide effort helps protect our mountain environment while providing valuable mulch material for residents.

Wood-Chipping Community Effort

  • Eligibility: All BMPOA property owners in good standing (dues/taxes paid) automatically eligible
  • Cost: Free service provided annually
  • Purpose: Reduce fire risk and provide sustainable disposal of brush and small trees
  • No Reservations: Simply place brush as directed—no advance registration needed

Program Schedule

  • Timing: Chipping typically occurs early spring (duration ~1 week)
  • Notification: Announced weeks in advance via BMPOA.org, newsletter, and Facebook group
  • Coordinator Contact: Email wood-chipping coordinator at jcook0313@gmail.com

B. BRUSH PILE GUIDELINES

Preparation Requirements

Placement

Place brush within 5 ft of roadside

Size Specifications

  • Branches ≤ 8″ diameter
  • ≤ 12 ft length
  • Cut branches into manageable sizes

Material Requirements

  • Clean wood/brush only
  • Remove rocks, metal, trash
  • No non-wood debris

Collection Strategy

Collect year-round to minimize accumulation and ensure manageable piles during chipping week

Proper Stacking

  • Stack loosely within 5 ft of roadway
  • Do not block roads or driveways
  • Ensure safe access for chipping equipment
  • Keep piles manageable in size

C. WOOD CHIP ACCESS & DELIVERY

Surplus Wood Chips

Surplus wood chips delivered free of charge to homeowner property during chipping week. Extra chips dumped at The Lodge and Deer Lake dam parking areas for pickup.

Free Delivery to Your Property

Email wood-chipping coordinator at jcook0313@gmail.com to schedule free delivery during chipping week

Self-Service Pickup Locations

  • The Lodge parking area - First-come, first-served
  • Deer Lake dam parking area - First-come, first-served

Bring your own containers and loading tools

Wood Chip Uses

  • Landscaping: Natural mulch for garden beds and around trees
  • Pathways: Natural walking surfaces for informal trails
  • Fire Prevention: Defensible space ground cover (less flammable than dried vegetation)
  • Soil Amendment: Decomposes over time to improve soil structure
  • Erosion Control: Helps prevent soil erosion on slopes

D. FIRE MITIGATION PARTNERSHIP

Virginia Department of Forestry Partnership

BMPOA partners with VA Dept. of Forestry for fire mitigation and evacuation planning.

Collaboration Areas

  • Defensible Space Guidance: Professional advice on creating fire-safe zones around homes
  • Evacuation Planning: Community-wide evacuation route planning and coordination
  • Fire Weather Monitoring: Access to professional fire weather forecasts and alerts
  • Technical Assistance: Expert guidance on fuel reduction projects

Community Coordination

  • BMPOA Roads Committee: Coordinates road access for evacuation
  • Public Safety Committee: Manages community evacuation zones
  • Fire Safety Committee: Organizes community fire prevention efforts

Community Benefits

  • Fire Risk Reduction: Removes flammable materials from around homes
  • Cost Savings: Free disposal of brush and small trees
  • Environmental Benefit: Sustainable use of natural materials
  • Community Cooperation: Neighbors working together for mutual safety
  • Professional Support: Access to state forestry expertise

Get Involved

Every property owner's participation makes our community safer. Start collecting brush throughout the year, and watch for announcements about the annual chipping schedule. Contact the wood-chipping coordinator with questions or to arrange chip delivery.
