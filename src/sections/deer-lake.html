B. LOCATION & ACCESS

The peaceful Deer Lake dock area provides access for fishing, kayaking, and quiet water recreation

Deer Lake serves as Blue Mountain's premier recreational facility, offering residents a beautiful mountain lake setting for water activities, fishing, and peaceful relaxation. The lake and surrounding facilities provide a central gathering place for community recreation.

Lake Features

  • Private mountain lake for Blue Mountain residents
  • Fishing dock with bench seating
  • Sandy beach area for swimming
  • Kayak and canoe launch area
  • Picnic tables and grilling facilities
  • Parking area near lake access

Activities Available

  • Fishing (bass, bluegill, and other species)
  • Swimming and wading
  • Kayaking and canoeing
  • Family picnics and gatherings
  • Wildlife observation
  • Photography and nature study

Getting to Deer Lake

Deer Lake is located within the Blue Mountain subdivision and is accessible via the community road system. The lake area includes designated parking and is within walking distance of most properties in the subdivision.

Access Details

  • Follow community roads to designated lake access area
  • Parking available for approximately 8-10 vehicles
  • Short walking path from parking to dock and beach
  • Accessible year-round, weather permitting

B. RECREATIONAL AREA PASSES

Access to Deer Lake is restricted to Blue Mountain property owners, their families, and authorized guests. A pass system helps manage usage and maintain the lake as a peaceful community amenity.

Property Owner Passes

  • Automatic Access: All current property owners have lake privileges
  • Family Members: Immediate family included in ownership privileges
  • No Fee: Lake access included in property ownership benefits
  • Year-Round: Access available all seasons, weather permitting

Guest Access

  • Accompanied Guests: Must be with property owner or family member
  • Day Use Only: Guest access limited to daylight hours
  • Responsibility: Property owner responsible for guest behavior
  • Guest Limits: Reasonable number of guests per property owner

Seasonal Access

Summer Season (May - September)

  • Full lake access and all amenities available
  • Swimming area maintained and monitored
  • Peak usage period - arrive early for best parking
  • Lake Committee actively maintains facilities

Winter Season (October - April)

  • Lake access available but limited amenities
  • No swimming area maintenance
  • Fishing still available (check ice conditions)
  • Snow may affect road access to parking area

C. LAKE & BEACH RULES

To ensure Deer Lake remains a safe and enjoyable facility for all residents, specific rules and guidelines govern its use. These rules help preserve water quality, protect wildlife, and maintain a peaceful environment.

Swimming & Water Safety

  • Swim at Your Own Risk: No lifeguard on duty - swimming is unsupervised
  • Children: Must be supervised by adults at all times
  • Hours: Daylight hours only - no night swimming
  • Weather: No swimming during storms or severe weather
  • Buddy System: Never swim alone - always have someone present
  • Emergency: Emergency contact information posted at lake area

Fishing Guidelines

  • Valid License: Virginia fishing license required for ages 16+
  • Catch and Release: Encouraged to maintain fish populations
  • Bait and Tackle: No live bait to prevent disease introduction
  • Dock Usage: Share dock space courteously with other anglers
  • Clean Up: Remove all fishing line and tackle
  • Quiet Hours: Respect early morning and evening quiet times

Environmental Protection

Deer Lake is a natural mountain lake ecosystem that requires protection to remain healthy and beautiful:

Water Quality

  • No soaps, shampoos, or chemicals in lake water
  • No feeding of waterfowl or fish
  • Report any water quality concerns to Lake Committee

Wildlife Respect

  • Observe wildlife from distance - do not approach or feed
  • Protect nesting areas during breeding seasons
  • Keep noise levels low to avoid disturbing animals
